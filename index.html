About

"If you do not conquer self, you will be conquered by self..."

  • 2005

    Beginnings

    Around the age of 10, it became clear that writing was not only a necessary skill needed to advance, but a passion that revealed itself through pen upon paper. At that moment it was decided that I would attend the prestegious Duke Ellington School of the Arts in Washington, D.C. with a concentration in Literary Media and Communication.

  • 2009-2013

    A Visionary is Born

    DESA allowed me many opportunities to explore myself as an artist, but realizing that my passion transcended beyond paper to visuals was the biggest gain of my high school experience. Here is when I began to view the world through lens-like eyes that saw an average day's scenery as the next scene of a movie. Entranced and intrigued, I was being molded, but not yet ready to take that leap.

  • 2013-2017

    What are you about?

    With free reign and the ability to explore as a film major, my creative muscles were tested, strengthed, and flexed across a forever widening spectrum. From producing to writing even entreprenuerial business classes and Russian language, the budding of a fruitful career begin to peak. Within four years, my expertise in all three stages of production excelled in front of and behind the camera, but I couldn't end there. Alongside film, I minored in New Media and Technology adding marketing, social media management, and community engagement to my resume.

  • Current

    Building of a brand

    From senior year of college onward, my sole purpose has been the creation and expansion of my brand. Through internships, production projects, and freelancing, I have built Imonie, the Media Entreprenuer, as an up-and-coming entity that is not only able to collaborate, but also work alone. Though I am just getting started, the versatility of my abilities and innovative work ethic promise a strong future.

NIGHTS

Written, Produced, Directed, Edited by Imonie King

Nights is a story which explores three entities encompassed in one common issue in the average household: lack of communication. Substance abuse as a coping mechanism, rage, and argueably the most important aspect, having that person to "lean on" can be seen throughout the short video, which doubles as a music video for the song Cage inspired by Washington, D.C. artist and native Zeroni.

LUCKY NUMBERS

Creatively Produced by Imonie King

Lucky Numbers is the story of a "down on his luck" gambling addict, who stumbles upon a Chineese mobster ring using their family restaurant as a front for money laundering. Absent mindedly he is given the "lucky numbers", which may lead to his financial big break or untimely demise.

THAT TIME IN THE ELEVATOR

Directed by Imonie King

Nothing can be worse than the actual break-up? Right? Well actually it can. That Time In The Elevator is the comedic story of two exes who get stuck on the elevator when one comes to pick up the remainder of their items from the other's apartment. No stone is left unturned as they wait it out for help, post break-up healing, or possibly both!
